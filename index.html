HINTS:

  • You have to keep playing till one of you Scores "5".
  • In other words, any one who scores "5" first is the OVERALL WINNER FOR THAT ROUND.
  • Start with Paper, since many people open with Rock.
  • Watch if your opponent repeats moves or cycles in order.
  • If they just won, they might stick with the same move.
  • If they just lost, they often switch to the move that beats yours.
  • Mix in randomness so you’re harder to predict.

RULES:

  • Rock beats Scissors by smashing them.
  • Scissors beat Paper by cutting it.
  • Paper beats Rock by covering it.
  • If both players choose the same move, it’s a tie.
  • Players reveal their choice at the same time.

INFO:

  • Rock-Paper-Scissors is believed to have originated in China over 2,000 years ago.
  • It’s often used as a simple decision-making tool, like flipping a coin.
  • Professional Rock-Paper-Scissors tournaments actually exist worldwide.
  • Strategies rely on psychology, probability, and spotting opponent patterns.
  • There are fun variations like Rock-Paper-Scissors-Lizard-Spock that add more moves.
